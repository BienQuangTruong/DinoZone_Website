tyrannosaurus

Tyrannosaurus was a bipedal carnivore with a massive skull balanced by a long, heavy tail. Powerful hind limbs, the forelimbs of were short but unusually powerful for their size, and they had two clawed digits. It was a quite smart and the largest apex predator.

CARNOTAURUS

Carnotaurus was a lightly built predator, measuring 7.5 to 9m long and weighing at 1.35 tons. The distinctive horns and the muscular neck may have been used in fighting it was adapted for running and was possibly one of the fastest large dinos.

DEINONYCHUS

This species, which could grow up to 3.4 meters (11 ft) long. Small body, sleek, horizontal posture, ratite-like spine, and especially the enlarged raptorial claws on the feet, which suggested an active, agile predator.

MEGALOSAURUS

Megalosaurus was about 6 metres, weighing about 700 kilograms. Walking on stout hindlimbs, its torso balanced by a tail. Its forelimbs were short, though very robust. Megalosaurus had a rather large head, long curved teeth. It was a robust and heavily muscled animal.

EORAPTOR

Eoraptor had a slender body that grew to about 1 metre in length, with an estimated weight of about 10 kilograms. Eoraptor is an omnivore, it was a swift sprinter and, upon catching its prey, it would use claws and teeth to tear the prey apart. It was one of the smartest in the Triassic.

TRICERATOPS

Triceratops is one of the most recognizable of all dinosaurs and the most well-known and also one of the largest, up to 9 meters long and 12 metric tons. The functions of the frills and three facial horns were used in fighting, species identification, courtship and dominance display.

ARGENTINOSAURUS

Argentinosaurus is one of the largest known land animals of all time, perhaps the largest, with length estimates ranging from 30 to 40 metres and weight estimates from 50 to 100 tonnes. A herbivore with the long neck which can be used to reach tree foliages and scnning the grounds.

THERIZINOSAURUS

Therizinosaurus was a colossal that could grow up to 9–10m long and weigh possibly over 3 tonnes. It is slow-moving, long-necked high browser equipped with a horny beak and a wide torso. The forelimbs were robust and had three fingers with long and sharp claws

ANKYLOSAURUS

Ankylosaurus had a robust broad body with wide, low skull, two horns pointing backward from the back of the head, its armor, consisting of knobs and plates of bone, or scutes, embedded in the skin. A herbivore with a sturdy armor, it has difficulties in finding food due to its wide body.

STEGOSAURUS

Stegosaurus were large, heavily built, herbivorous with rounded backs, long hind limbs, and tails held high in the air, upright plates and tail tipped with spikes. Their spiked tails were used for defense against predators, while their plates may have been used for thermoregulatory.

PTERANODON

Pteranodon is one of the largest pterosaur, with a horny toothless skull and wide wingspan capable of flying more quickly than any creature. Its diet is known to have included fish by dipping their beaks into the water while in low, soaring flight.

TROPEOGNATHUS

Tropeognathus is regarded as the largest pterosaur found in the Southern Hemisphere with the wingspan of 8.3-8.8 meters, 45 kg in weight and 2.4 meters length. The skull bore distinctive convex "keeled" crests on its snout and underside of the lower jaws, well-developed, and relatively large.

JEHOLOPTERUS

With its broad, short wings, Jeholopterus ningchengensis could bob and weave through branches and reeds to chase down insects. Once this agile hunter got close enough to its prey, it could snatch an insect right out of the air with its small jaws and tiny, sharp teeth.

Quetzalcoatlus

Quetzalcoatlus is an azhdarchid pterosaur known from the Late Cretaceous of North America (Maastrichtian stage). It is a member of the family Azhdarchidae, a family of advanced toothless pterosaurs with unusually long, stiffened necks.

Pliosaurus

Pliosaurus lived during the Late Jurassic Period and lived off of a diet of fish, squids and other marine reptiles. This beast was probably at the top of the food chain wherever it went due to its huge size of 10 to 13 metres and huge, powerful jaws with its rosette of large teeth at the tip of its snout.

Elasmosaurus

Elasmosaurus thrived during the Cretaceous Period, and lived on a diet of fish and other small sea creatures of that time. They typically reached around 10–14 meters in total body length, with a neck that counted for over half of its body length.

Mosasaurus

Mosasaurus was a genus of mosasaur, giant carnivorous, aquatic lizards, somewhat resembling flippered crocodiles, with big elongated jaws. Mosasaurus who was a generalist predator so it would take anything that fit into its mouth, not unlike tiger sharks today.

Spinosaurus

Spinosaurus were among the largest known of all carnivorous dinosaurs. The elongated neural spines, run to the end of the tail on both dorsal and ventral sides, could be a generalist predator that hunted variety of prey item, like aquatic animals such as massive, multi-ton fish species.
